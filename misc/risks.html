Risks and Threats of Artificial Intelligence



According to Cellan-Jones (2014, as cited in Boukerche et al., 2020), the use and creation of artificial intelligence, especially since computers can perform such abilities, this will cause artificial intelligence to exceed human intelligence. This could potentially surpass humans in every aspect and respect. The current pace of development of artificial intelligence indicated that the first breakthrough can happen in the years between 2045-2060. If such things occur, an avalanche changes will happen, with unpredictable consequences, because the extended knowledge and capabilities of artificial intelligence will be able to outdo people intellectually, and continually upgrade and enhance themselves (Fehler, 2017, as cited in Boukerche et al., 2020).

The main risk of artificial intelligence is that it could possibly replace human subjects, this brings the general problem of bringing people out of work. For example, companies that depending on artificial intelligence rely on decreasing the number human subjects or workers with whom they are involved. Therefore, this case brings major issues by the fact that it will reduce the number of workers who have the chance of benefitting from the employment opportunities (Nadimpalli, 2017).

Another misconception associated with artificial intelligence is that there is a chance of creating issue of replacing the intelligence of humans. This is expressed as an ethical case where more people would be relying and seek help from artificial intelligence, this issue was described as how humans will feel that their knowledge is not sufficient enough which therefore, should be replaced with the technologies of computers and machines. If this particular action is implemented human beings will lose the ability to handle various activities that would be purposeful or relevant to them. This process will also guide human beings to lose their general value towards humanity.
